LLOYDS BANK — ONLINE FOR BUSINESS

USER HELP & TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE

SECTION 1: REGISTRATION & FIRST-TIME LOGIN REQUIREMENTS

Guidance: Register for Online for Business Requirements

To register, you will need:

  - Your business name
  - Your Lloyds Business Account sort code
  - Your Lloyds Business Account account number
  - A mobile phone with a UK number
  - A valid passport or UK driving licence

Guidance: Register for Online for Business

If you have all the required registration details:

1.  Go to the Register your business for Online for Business page [2].
2.  Follow the on-screen instructions.

Guidance: First-Time Login Requirements (No Personal Account)

If you do not have a personal account with Lloyds, you must have the following
to log into Online for Business for the first time:

  - Username — Sent by post or email (takes up to 5 working days to arrive) [2].
  - Temporary password — Sent by text (takes up to 10 working days to arrive)
    [2].
  - Card reader — Sent by post (takes up to 10 working days to arrive) [2].
  - Authentication card or Business Debit Card — Sent by post (takes up to 5
    working days to arrive) [2].
  - PIN for your authentication card or Business Debit Card — Sent by post
    separately from your card (takes up to 10 working days to arrive) [2].

Note: All items will be sent separately after you have registered for Online for
Business [2].

Guidance: First-Time Login Requirements (Has Personal Account)

If you already have a personal account with Lloyds, you must have:

  - Card reader — Sent by post (takes up to 10 working days to arrive) [2].
  - Authentication card or Business Debit Card — Sent by post (takes up to 5
    working days to arrive) [2].
  - PIN for your authentication card or Business Debit Card — Sent by post
    separately from your card (takes up to 10 working days to arrive) [2].

SECTION 2: GENERAL LOGIN GUIDANCE

Guidance: Login Requirements

To log into Online for Business, you will need:

  - Your username [9].
  - A passkey, memorable information, or a card reader (using a Business Debit
    Card or authentication card and PIN) [9].

Guidance: Login on a Trusted Device and Using Passkey

Follow these steps to log in when you are on a trusted device and using a
passkey:

1.  Go to the Online for Business login page [9].
2.  Enter your username (previously called User ID) [9]. If you already use
    Lloyds personal internet banking, you may use the same login details [9].
3.  Select Passkey to confirm it is you on the Confirm it's you page [9].
4.  Select Continue on the Verify it's you with a passkey page [9].
5.  Follow the on-screen instructions [9].

Guidance: Login on a Trusted Device and Using Memorable Information

Follow these steps to log in when you are on a trusted device and using your
memorable information:

1.  Go to the Online for Business login page [9].
2.  Enter your username (previously called User ID) [7]. If you already use
    Lloyds personal internet banking, you may use the same login details [7].
3.  Select Memorable information to confirm it is you on the Confirm it's you
    page [7].
4.  Enter the three characters requested on the Enter your memorable information
    page [7].

Guidance: Login on a Trusted Device and Using Card Reader, Card, and PIN

Follow these steps to log in when you use your card reader, Business Debit Card
or authentication card, and PIN, and you are on a trusted device:

1.  Go to the Online for Business login page [7].
2.  Enter your username (previously called User ID) [7]. If you already use
    Lloyds personal internet banking, you may use the same login details [7].
3.  Select Card and reader to confirm it is you on the Confirm it's you page
    [7].
4.  Enter the last 5 digits of the card you are using on the Enter the passcode
    from your card reader page [7].
5.  Insert your Business Debit Card or authentication card into the card reader
    and press the Identify button [7].
6.  Enter the card PIN and press Enter on the card reader [7].
7.  Type the 8-digit passcode shown on the card reader into the Enter the
    passcode from your card reader page on screen and select Continue [7].

Guidance: Login NOT on a Trusted Device and Using Card and Reader

Follow these steps to log in when you use your card reader, Business Debit Card
or authentication card, and PIN, and you are not on a trusted device:

1.  Go to the Online for Business login page [7].
2.  Enter your username (previously called User ID) [7]. If you already use
    Lloyds personal internet banking, you may use the same login details [7].
3.  Enter the last 5 digits of the card you are using on the Enter the passcode
    from your card reader page [7].
4.  Insert your Business Debit Card or authentication card into the card reader
    and press the Identify button [7].
5.  Enter the card PIN and press Enter on the card reader [7].
6.  Type the 8-digit passcode shown on the card reader into the Enter the
    passcode from your card reader page on screen and select Continue [7].

SECTION 4: DETAILED TROUBLESHOOTING

Troubleshooting: What is Online for Business?

Online for Business is Lloyds Bank’s secure online banking service for business
customers [3]. It allows you to view and manage all of your business accounts in
one place [3].

Troubleshooting: User ID or Username

We have replaced the term "User ID" with "username" [8]. However, you might
still see the term "User ID" on some Lloyds Bank pages [8].

Troubleshooting: No UK Mobile Phone Number

If you do not have a mobile phone with a UK number, call us to register for
Online for Business on 0345 072 5555 [3].

  - Opening Hours: Monday to Friday, 8am to 6pm. Saturday, 9am to 2pm [3].
  - International Calls: +44 1733 347 338 [3].
  - Accessibility Support: Call via Relay UK if you have a hearing or speech
    impairment [3]. Sign Video services are also available for Deaf users who
    communicate using British Sign Language [3]. Additional information is
    available on the Deaf or Hearing Loss support page [3].

Troubleshooting: Username Isn't Working or Forgotten

If your username is not working or you have forgotten it, you will need to
recover it [8].

What you will need:

  - Your Lloyds Business Account sort code and account number, OR your Business
    Credit Card number [8].
  - Your first name [8].
  - Your last name [8].
  - Your date of birth [8].

Steps to recover:

1.  Go to the Forgotten your details? page [8].
2.  Enter your details on the Forgotten your logon details? page [8].
3.  Select your account type, then enter your sort code, account number, first
    name, last name, and date of birth [8].
4.  Select Continue to display your Username on screen [8].

Troubleshooting: No Username Received After Registering

If you are new to Lloyds Bank, have registered for Online for Business, and do
not have a personal account, your username is sent via email or post within 5
working days [3].

If you have waited more than 5 working days, call us on 0345 072 5555 (or
+44 1733 347 338 from outside the UK) [3].

Troubleshooting: No Temporary Password Received After Registering

If you are new to Lloyds Bank, have registered for Online for Business, and do
not have a personal account, your temporary password is sent by text within 10
working days [3].

If you have waited more than 10 working days, call us on 0345 072 5555 (or
+44 1733 347 338 from outside the UK) [3].

Troubleshooting: Lost a Temporary Password or It's Not Working

If you have lost your temporary password or it is not working when logging in
for the first time, you can create a new password [3].

What you will need:

  - Your username (User ID) [3]
  - Your first name [4]
  - Your last name [4]
  - Your date of birth [4]

Note: The recovery process differs depending on whether you have a working card
reader, Business Debit Card or authentication card, and your PIN [4].

Option A: If you have your username, card reader, card, and PIN:

1.  Go to the Forgotten your details? page [4].
2.  Enter your User ID, first name, last name, and date of birth, then select
    Continue [4].
3.  Go to the Password & memorable information section on the What are you
    having problems with today? page and select Continue [4].
4.  Select Yes on the Do you have a working card reader with you? page [4].
5.  Select Yes, I want to reset my password on the Do you want to reset your
    password? page [4].
6.  Enter and confirm your new password [4]. Your password must be 8 to 15
    characters, have no spaces, and contain at least 3 letters and 1 number [4].
    Allowed special characters are: @ . & - + / ' , _ [4]
7.  Select either Yes, I want to reset my memorable information or No, I know my
    memorable information [4].
8.  Enter the last 5 digits of the card you are using on the Enter the passcode
    from your card reader page [4].
9.  Insert your Business Debit Card or authentication card into the card reader
    and press Identify [4].
10. Enter the card PIN and press Enter on the card reader [4].
11. Type the 8-digit passcode shown on the card reader into the field on your
    screen and select Continue [4].

Option B: If you have your username, but DO NOT have your card reader, card, or PIN:

1.  Go to the Forgotten your details? page [4].
2.  Enter your User ID, first name, last name, and date of birth, then select
    Continue [4].
3.  Go to the Password & memorable information section on the What are you
    having problems with today? page and select Continue [4].
4.  Select No on the Do you have a working card reader with you? page [4].
5.  Select Yes, I want to reset my password on the Do you want to reset your
    password? page [4].
6.  Confirm the reset option [4].
7.  Enter and confirm your new password (8 to 15 characters, no spaces, at
    least 3 letters, 1 number; allowed special characters: @ . & - + / ' , _)
    [4].
8.  Select either Yes, I want to reset my memorable information or No, I know my
    memorable information [4].
9.  Call us on 0345 072 5555 (or +44 1733 347 338 from outside the UK) to
    complete the security verification [4].

Troubleshooting: Memorable Information Isn't Working or Forgotten

You do not need memorable information to log in; you can use your passkey or a
card reader setup instead [8]. If you wish to reset your memorable information:

What you will need:

  - Your username (User ID) [8]
  - Your first name [8]
  - Your last name [8]
  - Your date of birth [8]

Option A: If you have a card reader, Business Debit Card or authentication card, and PIN:

1.  Go to the Forgotten your details? page [8].
2.  Enter your User ID, first name, last name, and date of birth [8].
3.  Under What are you having problems with today?, go to Password & memorable
    information and select Continue [10].
4.  Select Yes on the Do you have a working card reader with you? page [10].
5.  Select No, I know my password on the Do you want to reset your password?
    page [10].
6.  Select Yes, I want to reset my memorable information [10].
7.  Create your memorable information when prompted [10]. It must be 6 to 15
    characters with no spaces, contain at least 3 letters and 1 number, and have
    no special characters [10].
8.  Enter the last 5 digits of your card on the Enter the passcode from your
    card reader page [10].
9.  Insert your card into the card reader and press Identify [10].
10. Enter the card PIN and press Enter [10].
11. Enter the 8-digit passcode displayed on the card reader on your screen and
    select Continue [10].

Option B: If you DO NOT have a card reader, card, or PIN:

1.  Go to the Forgotten your details? page [10].
2.  Enter your User ID, first name, last name, and date of birth [10].
3.  Select No on the Do you have a working card reader with you? page [10].
4.  Select I want to reset my logon details without my card reader on the What
    would you like to do? page [10].
5.  Select No, I know my password on the Do you want to reset your password?
    page [10].
6.  Select Yes, I want to reset my memorable information [10].
7.  Create your memorable information (6 to 15 characters, no spaces, at least 3
    letters, 1 number, no special characters) [10].
8.  Call us on 0345 072 5555 (or +44 1733 347 338 from outside the UK) [10].
9.  Quote the reference number displayed on the Please call us to confirm your
    changes page to the agent [10].

Troubleshooting: No Passkey (Passkey and Everyday Login)

You do not need a passkey to log into Online for Business [14]. You can instead
log in using memorable information on a trusted device, or by using your card
reader setup [14].

If you do not have a passkey, we will invite you to set one up on any secure
device you use to log in [14].

SECTION 5: CARDS, PINs & HARDWARE SUPPORT

Troubleshooting: What is a Trusted Device?

A trusted device is a phone, tablet, or computer you have marked as safe [10].
This lets the system know it is really you and prevents extra security checks
during login [10].

You can only trust your device when prompted by the system during a normal login
flow [10].

Supported browsers for trusting a device include:

  - Google Chrome (Desktop & Mobile) — V66 and above [11]
  - Microsoft Edge (Desktop) — V13 and above [11]
  - Mozilla Firefox (Desktop & Mobile) — V34 and above [11]
  - Opera (Desktop & Mobile) — V24 and above [11]
  - Safari (Desktop & Mobile) — V11 and above [11]
  - Yandex (Desktop & Mobile) — V15.5 and above [11]

Troubleshooting: System Not Asking for Memorable Information (on a Trusted Device)

If the system is not offering memorable information as a login option on a
device you previously trusted, it may be due to:

  - Browser cookies being cleared (manually or automatically) [11].
  - Using a private or incognito browsing session [11].
  - Unusual login activity, or multiple users logging in on the same device
    [11].

We will prompt you to trust your device again once we identify a regular and
safe pattern of use [11].

Troubleshooting: What is a Card Reader?

A card reader is a secure physical device used to access Online for Business and
to authorize sensitive transactions [11]. It does not store any personal
information [11].

A card reader passcode is required for:

  - Person-to-person payments [11]
  - All bill payments [11]
  - New standing orders [11]
  - International payments [11]
  - Registering the mobile app for the first time [11]

Note: Card readers are universal [11]. You can use a card reader from another
bank as long as it has three buttons: Identify, Respond, and Sign [11].

Troubleshooting: Card Reader Won't Turn On / Needs Replacement

If your card reader does not turn on, the batteries likely need replacing
[11, 13].

  - Battery Type: Four (4) LR44 batteries [13].
  - Instructions: Open the battery compartment on the back of the card reader to
    replace them [11, 13].

If the card reader still does not work after replacing the batteries:

  - Via Mobile App: Go to Support > Message us to request a replacement [13].
  - Via Website: Visit the Order a New Business Card Reader page and follow the
    on-screen steps [13].

Note: Lloyds provides up to 3 free card readers to each customer [13].
Replacements are delivered to your registered business mailing address within 7
to 10 working days [13].

Troubleshooting: Card Reader is Locked

If your card reader display shows "card locked contact bank", this means your
card reader (not your actual bank card) has been locked [13].

To unlock it, call us on 0345 072 5555 (or +44 1733 347 338 from outside the UK)
[13].

Troubleshooting: Card Reader Display is Too Small

If the characters on your card reader's screen are too small, press the '0'
(zero) button on the keypad to enlarge the text [13].

Troubleshooting: What is a Passcode?

A card reader passcode is a one-time 8-digit code generated by inserting your
bank card, pressing Identify, and entering your card's PIN [13].

Troubleshooting: "Last 5 Digits Not Matching" Message

If you receive a message stating the last 5 digits of your card do not match
during login, verify that you are using the correct card [13]. Check the card
number against the digit requirements displayed on Step 1 of the login screen
[13].

Troubleshooting: Lost or Forgotten Business Debit Card PIN

You do not need your card PIN to log into Online for Business if you use a
passkey or memorable information on a trusted device [12].

If you need your Business Debit Card PIN:

  - Via Mobile App: If you are registered on the business banking app, you can
    view your PIN securely in the Cards section [12].
  - If you do not have the App: Call us on 0345 072 5555 to request a paper PIN
    replacement [12].
  - Activation: Once your replacement PIN arrives by post, you must activate it
    at any Lloyds, Bank of Scotland, or Halifax ATM [12]. Insert the card, enter
    the PIN, and select Services > Unlock PIN [12].

Troubleshooting: Lost or Forgotten Authentication Card PIN

Authentication card PINs cannot be viewed in the mobile app [12]. If forgotten,
you must request a replacement [12].

1.  Call us on 0345 072 5555 (or +44 1733 347 338 from outside the UK) to
    request a replacement PIN [12].
2.  Once received by post, activate it at any Lloyds, Bank of Scotland, or
    Halifax ATM by selecting Services > Unlock PIN [12].

Note: Authentication card PINs cannot be activated at Lloyds Bank Market Street
(Manchester) or Bank of Scotland Argyle Street (Glasgow) ATMs [12].

Troubleshooting: Lost or Forgotten Business Debit Card

If your card has been lost or stolen:

1.  Report it immediately by calling 0345 072 5555 (or +44 1733 347 338 from
    outside the UK) [14].
2.  Order a replacement card via the Order a Replacement Business Debit Card
    page [14]. Only the registered cardholder can order a replacement [14].
3.  Activate your new card upon arrival by completing a standard Chip & PIN
    transaction [14].

Troubleshooting: Lost or Forgotten Authentication Card

If you lose your authentication card, you can still log in using a passkey or
memorable information on a trusted device [14]. To order a replacement card:

  - Via Mobile App: Go to Support > Message us and ask the digital assistant to
    "Order a replacement authentication card" [14].
  - If you do not have the App: Call us on 0345 072 5555 to place an order [14].

Note: Once your new authentication card arrives, you can use it immediately with
your existing card PIN without needing to activate it [14].
